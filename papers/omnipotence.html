Omnipotence paradox: an alternative solution

Kalim Chhoun · 30 December 2024

Philosophy Logic Paradox

The omnipotence paradox asks whether an all-powerful being can create a stone so heavy that even it cannot lift it. We propose an alternative resolution: neutralising the paradox by introducing a second, structurally symmetric paradox.

The paradox

Can God — an omnipotent being — create a stone so heavy that no one, including Himself, can lift it?

This statement is paradoxical on the grounds that:

  • If God could create the stone, He would not be able to lift it. Therefore, God is not omnipotent.
  • If God could not create the stone, He is not omnipotent.

Proposed solution

To resolve this paradox, we must turn it into another paradox.

God must first split Himself into two. Then, He could create the stone. One version of God would be able to lift the stone, while the other would not. This way, the new paradox voids the initial paradox. Hence, the omnipotence of God stays intact.
