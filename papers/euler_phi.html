Euler's phi function: the sum over divisors

Kalim Chhoun · 23 January 2025

Number Theory Elementary Proof Euler's Totient

We prove that the sum of Euler's totient function over all divisors of \( m \) equals \( m \): \( \sum_{d \mid m} \varphi(d) = m \) for all \( m \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \). The proof proceeds via two lemmas on prime powers, then applies multiplicativity through prime factorisation.

Main result

Theorem

For all \( m \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \): \[ \sum_{d \mid m} \varphi(d) = m \]

Lemmas

Lemma 1

For any prime \( p \) and \( n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \): \( \varphi(p^n) = p^n - p^{n-1} \)

Proof

The integers \( a \) with \( 1 \leq a \leq p^n \) that fail to be coprime to \( p^n \) are exactly those divisible by \( p \): there are \( p^{n-1} \) of them. So \( \varphi(p^n) = p^n - p^{n-1} \).

Lemma 2

For any prime \( p \) and \( n \in \mathbb{Z}^+ \): \( \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n} \varphi(p^i) = p^n \)

Proof

From Lemma 1, the values \( \varphi(p^i) = p^i - p^{i-1} \) telescope:

\[ \varphi(1) + \varphi(p) + \varphi(p^2) + \cdots + \varphi(p^n) = 1 + (p-1) + (p^2-p) + \cdots + (p^n - p^{n-1}) = p^n \]

\( \therefore\ \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}\varphi(p^i) = p^n \)

Proof of main theorem

Proof

Write \( m = \prod_{i=1}^{s} p_i^{k_i} \). By Lemma 2 applied to each prime power:

\[ \sum_{d \mid p_1^{k_1}} \varphi(d) = p_1^{k_1}, \quad \sum_{d \mid p_2^{k_2}} \varphi(d) = p_2^{k_2}, \quad \ldots, \quad \sum_{d \mid p_s^{k_s}} \varphi(d) = p_s^{k_s} \]

By multiplicativity of \( \varphi \):

\[ \sum_{d \mid m} \varphi(d) = \prod_{i=1}^{s}\left(\sum_{d \mid p_i^{k_i}} \varphi(d)\right) = \prod_{i=1}^{s} p_i^{k_i} = m \]

\( \therefore\ \displaystyle\sum_{d \mid m} \varphi(d) = m \)

Remark — combinatorial interpretation

The integers from \( 1 \) to \( m \) can be partitioned by \( \gcd(k, m) \). For each divisor \( d \mid m \), exactly \( \varphi(d) \) integers \( k \in \{1,\ldots,m\} \) satisfy \( \gcd(k,m) = m/d \). Summing over all divisors recovers \( m \).
